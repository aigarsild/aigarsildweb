Brand Identity

This is what I do at the core. I design the visual foundation of your company — the things that make people recognize you and take you seriously.

A brand identity project with me typically includes:

  • Logo design — primary mark, secondary versions, icon
  • Color palette with clear usage rules
  • Typography selection and hierarchy
  • Visual guidelines your team can actually follow
  • Core messaging direction

The goal is simple: when someone sees your website, your social post, your business card, or your product — it all looks like it belongs together. I keep guidelines practical and short, because a brand system only works if people actually use it.

Website, Webshop & Product

Most of my clients don't just need a brand — they need it applied somewhere. Because I design and build, your brand goes from concept to a live website, webshop, or product interface without anything getting lost in translation.

I build on whatever platform fits: custom code for marketing sites, WordPress for content-heavy sites, WooCommerce for shops. I've also built full SaaS products from the ground up.

AI Brand Kit

Every brand project includes a reference document your team can use with ChatGPT or Claude to create on-brand content — social posts, page copy, emails — without design help. It captures your voice, tone, and visual rules so anyone on your team can stay consistent.

Ongoing Support

After we're done, I stay available. I set up a simple ticket system for change requests, and most clients keep me on for smaller updates and improvements over time. No chasing emails, no lost requests.

How it usually goes

We start with a short call. I learn about your company, your audience, and what you're trying to communicate. From there I develop the brand direction — logo concepts, color palette, typography. You see progress along the way and we refine together until it feels right. If you also need a website or product built from the brand, that follows naturally. Most projects take 2–4 weeks depending on scope.
